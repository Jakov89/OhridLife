Ohrid's designation as the "Jerusalem of the Balkans" stems from its incredible collection of 365 churches‚Äîone for each day of the year. This remarkable concentration of sacred architecture spans over a millennium, from the 9th century foundations of Saint Clement's monastery to the medieval masterpieces that crown the city's hills.

From the Byzantine frescoes of Saint Sophia to the clifftop drama of Saint John at Kaneo, these sacred spaces have witnessed the rise and fall of empires, the birth of alphabets, and the prayers of countless pilgrims.
